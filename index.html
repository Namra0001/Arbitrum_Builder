Understanding Layer 2 & Arbitrum

Why Ethereum Needed Layer 2

Ethereum mainnet can only handle about 15 transactions per second. As DeFi, NFTs, and dApps exploded in popularity, network congestion drove gas fees to $50+ per transaction. Layer 2 solutions were built to scale Ethereum without sacrificing its decentralization or security — handling the heavy lifting off-chain while anchoring results back to L1.

What Is Arbitrum?

Arbitrum is an optimistic rollup — a type of Layer 2 that runs an EVM-compatible chain alongside Ethereum. It executes transactions off-chain, bundles them into batches, and posts compressed data to Ethereum. Smart contracts on Arbitrum work exactly like on mainnet, so developers can deploy with zero code changes.

Real-World Benefit

GMX, a leading decentralized exchange, processes billions in trading volume on Arbitrum. Users swap tokens for less than $0.05 in fees versus $5–$20 on Ethereum mainnet — a 100x cost reduction that makes high-frequency DeFi trading practical for everyday users.
